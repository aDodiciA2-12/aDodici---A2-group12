As it stands today Cyber threats are growing more and more common as we edge towards a world enriched in technology, we have become dependent on our devices for almost everything making Cyber attacks a very real and sobering reality. How will we be combating this threat in the future? One way is through the use of artificial intelligence (AI), Chuck Brooks, a world leader in Cybersecurity and emerging technologies describes AI in Cybersecurity as a, “technology that appears to emulate human performance typically by learning, coming to its own conclusions, appearing to understand complex content, engaging in natural dialogues with people, enhancing human cognitive performance or replacing people on execution of non-routine tasks”[3]. So what does this mean? It means computers will likely one day soon be sentient and will have cognitive autonomy, they will posses the ability to analyse, learn, detect and mitigate any upcoming suspected Cyber attacks.

One technology helping drive the rise of AI is quantum computing, what is this new form of computing?, quantum computers use a new mode of computing based on ‘qubits’, instead of regular bits. Qubits are more workable than bits because they can be turned on, off or in a state of ‘superposed’ which is basically a state of limbo[4], think of Schrödinger’s Cat neither dead or alive. What does this mean? I have no idea and you would be forgiven to not understand as well, this is quantum mechanics after all. So how does AI, quantum computing and Cybersecurity tie together? With the use of these super computers we can advance AI to unimaginable heights creating methods and solving many problems we face today in all aspects of life let alone Cybersecurity. An example of the incredible power quantum computing will provide was seen back in October of 2019, researchers from the University of California, Santa Barbra used 53 entangled qubits to solve a problem with a quantum computer in just 200 seconds, this same problem would have taken a super computer 10,000 years to solve[4]. Having this incredible computing power will help AI learn and adapt faster than humans and regular computers can, there is of course one down side to this and that is if this technology gets into the wrong hands. Modern day encryption practices will be made redundant if these computers can decipher codes in minutes or perhaps instantly instead of years, luckily there might be some ways around this using quantum key distribution (QKD), this uses quantum physics to build an encryption key[4].
